Kare Kare

Kare-Kare is a popular Filipino stew known for its rich and savory peanut sauce. Traditionally made with beef tripe and ox tail. Kare-Kare is often enjoyed with a side of bagoong, a shrimp paste condiment, and is a highlight of Filipino feasts and celebrations.

prep time

15 min.

cook time

1 hr.

serving

1 serving

Tags : beef lunch

instructions

step 1

In a small bowl, soak the annatto seeds in hot water for 10 minutes. Drain and set aside.

step 2

Heat cooking oil in a pot over medium heat. Saut√© chopped onion and minced garlic until fragrant and translucent.

step 3

Add the beef tripe to the pot and cook until slightly browned.

step 4

Dissolve the peanut butter in water, then pour it into the pot. Stir well to combine.

step 5

Add the annatto seeds and simmer over low heat for about 30 minutes or until the sauce thickens.

step 6

Add the sliced eggplant and banana blossom to the pot. Continue simmering for another 10 minutes or until the vegetables are cooked.

step 7

Season with salt and pepper to taste. Adjust the seasoning according to your preference.

step 8

Remove from heat and transfer the Beef Kare-Kare to a serving plate.

step 9

Serve hot with steamed rice and bagoong (shrimp paste) on the side.
