Baked Tahong

Sarciadong Galunggong is a popular Filipino dish that showcases the use of galunggong, a type of fish abundant in Philippine waters.

prep time

15 min.

cook time

12 min.

serving

1 serving

Tags : seafood

instructions

step 1

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) or you can use an air fryer.

step 2

Clean the mussels thoroughly, scrubbing them under running water and removing any beards or debris. Discard any mussels that are open or cracked.

step 3

In a small bowl, combine the softened butter, grated cheese, minced garlic, chopped parsley (if using), salt, and pepper. Mix well to create a flavorful butter mixture.

step 4

Place the cleaned mussels on a baking tray, with the flat side facing up.

step 5

Spoon a small amount of the butter mixture onto each mussel, distributing it evenly.

step 6

Once all the mussels are topped with the butter mixture, place the baking tray in the preheated oven.

step 7

Bake the mussels for about 10-12 minutes or until the butter has melted and the cheese is golden and bubbly.

step 8

Remove the tray from the oven and let the mussels cool for a few minutes.

step 8

Serve the Baked Tahong hot as an appetizer or main dish. Squeeze lemon wedges over the mussels for a tangy touch (if desired).
