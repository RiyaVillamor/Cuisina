Sinigang na Bangus

The dish features the delicate and flaky milkfish, a staple in Philippine cuisine, and the tamarind-infused soup creates a refreshing and tangy flavor profile.

prep time

15 min.

cook time

20 min.

serving

1 serving

Tags : seafood

instructions

step 1

In a pot, bring the water to a boil over medium heat.

step 2

Add the tomatoes and onions to the boiling water and let it simmer for a few minutes until softened.

step 3

Add the radish, eggplant, and okra to the pot. Simmer for about 5 minutes until the vegetables are tender.

step 4

Place the Bangus fillet into the pot and gently push it into the broth.

step 5

Stir in the tamarind powder or sinigang mix. Adjust the amount based on desired sourness.

step 6

Add the green chili pepper (if using) for an extra kick of spiciness.

step 7

Season the broth with fish sauce, salt, and pepper according to taste.

step 8

Continue simmering the sinigang for about 5-7 minutes or until the Bangus fillet is cooked through.

step 9

Remove the pot from heat and let it rest for a few minutes.

step 10

Serve the Sinigang na Bangus hot with steamed rice. Garnish with fresh spinach or kangkong leaves if desired.
