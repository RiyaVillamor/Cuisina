Sinigang na Baboy

Pork Sinigang is a classic Filipino soup known for its tangy and savory flavors. It is typically made with tamarind, which gives it a distinct sour taste. The dish often features a combination of pork, vegetables, and a variety of local greens, making it a comforting and nutritious meal in Filipino cuisine.

prep time

10 min.

cook time

40 min.

serving

1 serving

Tags : pork

instructions

step 1

In a pot, boil the water. Once boiling, add the pork ribs and cook until tender, about 30 minutes.

step 2

Add the onions, garlic, and tomatoes to the pot. Simmer for 5 minutes or until the vegetables soften.

step 3

Dissolve the tamarind powder or sinigang mix in a small amount of water, then pour it into the pot. Stir well.

step 4

Add the radish and simmer for 5 minutes.

step 5

Add the eggplant and continue simmering for another 5 minutes.

step 6

Season with fish sauce or salt and pepper to taste.

step 7

Add the kangkong leaves and tender stems. Cook for another 2 minutes or until wilted

step 8

Remove from heat and transfer the Pork Sinigang to a serving bowl.

step 9

Serve hot with steamed rice.
