Dinakdakan

Dinakdakan is a popular dish from the Ilocos region of the Philippines. It is made with grilled pork, typically the face or ears, and flavored with a tangy and creamy dressing. Dinakdakan is often enjoyed as a pulutan (appetizer) and is best paired with cold beer or rice wine.

prep time

20 min.

cook time

15 min.

serving

1 serving

Tags : beef seafood veggies pork

instructions

step 1

Boil the pork belly in water until tender. Once cooked, grill the pork over charcoal or on a grill pan until nicely browned. Chop the grilled pork into small pieces.

step 2

In a mixing bowl, combine the chopped pork, red onion, green chili pepper, and red chili pepper.

step 3

In a separate bowl, mix calamansi juice or lime juice, vinegar, mayonnaise, salt, and pepper to make the dressing.

step 4

Pour the dressing over the pork mixture. Toss well to coat the ingredients evenly.

step 5

Adjust the seasoning according to your preference, adding more salt, pepper, or calamansi juice if desired.

step 6

Transfer the Dinakdakan to a serving plate.

step 7

Serve chilled as an appetizer or side dish.
