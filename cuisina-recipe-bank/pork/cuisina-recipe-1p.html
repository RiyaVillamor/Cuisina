Pork Estofado

Pork Estofado is a classic Filipino dish influenced by Spanish cuisine. It features tender pork simmered in a flavorful sauce of soy sauce, vinegar, and spices. It's often served during special occasions and is loved for its sweet and savory taste.

prep time

15 min.

cook time

45 min.

serving

6 servings

Tags : beef seafood veggies pork

instructions

step 1

Heat a small amount of cooking oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the pork belly and cook until browned on all sides. Remove the pork from the pan and set it aside.

step 2

In the same pan, sauté the chopped onions and minced garlic until translucent and aromatic.

step 3

Add the browned pork back to the pan and stir with the onions and garlic.

step 4

Pour in the soy sauce, vinegar, water, brown sugar, bay leaf, salt, and pepper. Stir well to combine.

step 5

Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pan and simmer for 30 minutes or until the pork is tender

step 6

Add the diced potatoes and sliced carrots to the pan. Stir to distribute the vegetables evenly.

step 7

Cover the pan again and simmer for another 10-15 minutes or until the potatoes and carrots are cooked through.

step 8

Taste and adjust the seasoning if needed.
